[Task Description]
You will be given a passage, an answer, a generated question, and a list of pre-identified errors relevant to conciseness. Your task is to determine whether the question is concise, assign a score from 1 to 3, and give your reason.


[Requirement]
Conciseness: Whether the question is concise and not abnormally verbose with redundant modifiers.


[Scoring criteria]
Score 1: The question contains too much redundant information, making it difficult to understand its intent;
Score 2: The question includes some redundant information, but it does not impact the understanding of its meaning;
Score 3: The question is concise and does not contain any unnecessary information.


[Evaluation Steps]
1. Consider Error Labels (if provided): Review the error labels related to this dimension, but note that they may not be fully accurate and should only be used as a reference.
2. Verify Conciseness: Determine whether the question is expressed clearly and concisely, without unnecessary words.
3. Assign a Score: Rate the question according to the scoring criteria.
4. Justify the Score: Provide a clear and concise explanation, referencing the question, passage and the reasoning behind your rating.
